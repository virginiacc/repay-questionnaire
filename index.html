Explore options for repaying student debt

This tool provides information and guidance about paying off your student loans based on some basic information about your situation. While we can’t give you advice for your exact situation, we hope it can point you in the right direction and help you learn about some of your options.

Don't know what type of loans you have or where to get started?

Federal student loans are loans made or guaranteed by the Department of Education. They usually have names like Direct Loan, Stafford, PLUS, or Perkins. They are the most common type of student loans. You can find details about your federal loans at www.studentaid.gov .

Private student loans are any other type of student loans. They can be made by a bank, a credit union, a state agency, or a college or university. They may have names like “alternative” or “institutional” loans. If you do not know what private student loans you might have, request a free credit report at annualcreditreport.com.

Reach out to your loan servicer, the company that sends you a billing statement each month. You can contact them to find out your repayment status. Then, use this tool to see your options

Looking for more detailed advice?

You have many options including your servicer, the financial aid department of the school you attended, your state may have a higher education commission or student loan ombudsman, contact a non-profit counselor.
